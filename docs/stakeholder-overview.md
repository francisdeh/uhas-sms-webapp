# UHAS Basic School — Digital School Management System
### Stakeholder Overview | April 2026

---

## What We Are Building

UHAS Basic School is getting a purpose-built digital management system — designed specifically for how the school operates, from KG through JHS 3.

The system replaces manual registers, paper forms, and spreadsheets with a single, secure web platform that every member of staff can use from their phone or computer. Parents get their own access too.

Everything — from taking attendance to publishing exam results — happens in one place.

---

## Who the System Is For

The system is designed around the actual structure of the school. Each person sees only what is relevant to their role.

| Person | What They Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Head of School / Admin** | Full visibility — manage students, staff, results, announcements, and reports |
| **Deputy Head** | Manage and monitor their division (JHS, Primary, or KG) — staff attendance, lesson plan approvals |
| **Subject Head (HOD)** | First reviewer for JHS lesson plans; view subject coverage across classes |
| **Class Teacher** | Take daily student attendance, enter exam scores, write and submit lesson plans |
| **Parent / Guardian** | View their child's attendance and results from any device |

---

## Progress So Far

The system is being built in phases. Here is where we stand today.

---

### ✅ Phase 1 — Login & Access Control

Every user has a personal account with a role assigned by the Admin.

- Secure login page with password reset support
- Each role is automatically directed to their own dashboard after login
- Accounts can be created, deactivated, or reactivated by the Admin
- Staff receive an invite link when their account is created — no manual password sharing

**Why it matters:** No one can access data that is not relevant to their role. The school's information is protected.

---

### ✅ Phase 2 — Student & Staff Records

A complete digital register for every student and every staff member.

**Students:**
- Register new students with all personal details — name, date of birth, guardian contacts, nationality, religion, and more
- Every student gets an automatically generated Student ID
- Students can be transferred between classes within the same academic year
- Printable student ID cards generated directly from the system
- Students who leave can be deactivated — their records are never deleted

**Staff:**
- Register all staff with contact details and their assigned role
- Staff profiles show their division, current assignment, and status
- Roles can be updated as staff responsibilities change

**Why it matters:** No more lost paper forms. Every student and staff record is accurate, searchable, and always available.

---

### ✅ Phase 3 — Classes, Subjects & Teacher Assignments

A clear picture of who teaches what, and where.

- All classes are set up per academic year — KG 1, KG 2, Primary 1 through 6, and JHS 1 through 3
- Every subject is linked to the classes where it is taught
- Each subject in each class has a teacher assigned to it
- A class teacher is designated per class for attendance purposes

**Why it matters:** The Admin and Deputy Heads can immediately see which classes have teachers assigned and which do not — no guessing, no calls.

---

### ✅ Phase 4 — Attendance

Daily attendance, tracked digitally — for both students and staff.

**Student Attendance:**
- Class teachers mark each student as present, absent, or late every morning
- Records are locked after the school day — no backdating without Admin approval
- Attendance history is viewable at any time

**Staff Attendance:**
- Deputy Heads record which staff in their division were present each day
- Staff can submit leave requests (sick leave, maternity, personal, or other) directly through the system
- Deputy Heads approve or reject leave requests — all in one place

**Parent View:**
- Parents can log in and see their child's attendance for any day or month
- A calendar view shows at a glance which days were present, absent, or late

**Why it matters:** Attendance has always been the hardest thing to track consistently. This makes it automatic, accurate, and visible to everyone who needs to see it.

## What's Coming Next

The following features are designed and ready to be built. They follow the same quality bar as everything completed so far.

---

### 🔜 Phase 5 — Exam Scores & Report Cards

This phase brings the entire results process into the system.

- Teachers enter class scores and exam scores for their subjects
- The system automatically calculates total scores and grades using the Ghana Education Service (GES) grading scale
- Report cards are generated digitally — with scores, grades, teacher comments, and the Head's remarks
- The Head of School reviews report cards before they are published
- Parents can view their child's results as soon as they are published
- Results from previous terms are preserved for tracking progress over time

---

### 🔜 Phase 6 — Lesson Plans & Scheme of Work

A structured workflow for lesson plan submission and approval.

- Teachers write and upload their weekly lesson plans and schemes of work
- Plans follow the school's approval chain:
  - JHS: Teacher → Subject Head → Deputy Head
  - Primary and KG: Teacher → Deputy Head
- Reviewers can approve, request changes, or reject with a written comment
- Teachers receive notifications when their plan status changes
- The Deputy Head can see at a glance which teachers have submitted for the week

---

### 🔜 Phase 7 — Announcements & Communication

Keeping everyone informed — staff, teachers, and parents.

- Admin sends school-wide announcements to all users
- Deputy Heads send division-level notices
- Important announcements trigger email notifications to relevant recipients
- Parents are notified when results are published or when their child's attendance drops below a threshold
- Report cards are emailed to parents at the end of each term

---

### 🔜 Phase 8 — Reports & Analytics

A clear view of how the school is performing.

- Dashboard summaries for each role — today's attendance rate, pending approvals, recent announcements
- Student performance reports — average scores by subject, pass/fail rates, top performers
- Attendance summaries by class, division, and date range
- Lesson plan submission compliance — who has submitted for the current week
- PSC report — total student population, breakdown by class and gender, teacher counts per department
- All reports can be printed or exported as PDF

## Key Principles Behind the Design

**Built for the school's real structure.** Every screen reflects how UHAS Basic School actually works — divisions, roles, and the approval chain are all modelled on the school's own organisation.

**Works on any device.** Teachers can mark attendance on a phone. The Admin can manage student records on a laptop. The system adjusts to any screen size.

**Nothing is ever deleted.** Student and staff records are deactivated, not removed. This means the school keeps a complete historical record — important for reports, promotions, and audits.

**Data stays private.** Each person only sees what they need. A teacher cannot see another teacher's classes. A parent sees only their own child.

**Designed to grow.** Features being built today will connect seamlessly with exam results, lesson plans, and reports in the phases that follow.
